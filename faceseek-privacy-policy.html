FaceSeek Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 23, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the FaceSeek app ("FaceSeek", "we", "us") handles information when you use it. FaceSeek is a reverse-image search tool: you choose a photo, and the app finds public web pages where a visually similar image appears. By using FaceSeek you agree to this policy.

In short: FaceSeek does not perform facial recognition, does not identify people, and does not build a profile of anyone. The only face-related processing is on-device and is never transmitted. To run a search, the photo you choose is sent to Google Cloud Vision to find where that image appears online — and we ask for your explicit permission before the first time this happens.

1. Face data (what we do and do not do)

Face data is sensitive, so we want to be precise about it:

  • On-device face landmarks only. Before a search, FaceSeek may use Apple's on-device Vision framework to detect face-landmark points (eyes, nose, jaw outline, etc.) purely to display the "analyzing" animation. This processing happens entirely on your device.
  • Nothing is transmitted. Those landmark points are never saved and never leave your device.
  • No faceprint, no recognition. We do not create, store, share, sell, or use any facial-recognition template, faceprint, face embedding, or other biometric identifier. We do not match your face against any database and we do not use face data to identify you or anyone else.
  • What is actually sent for the search. When you start a search, the photo you selected (an ordinary image, not a face template) is sent to Google Cloud Vision to find visually similar public images. See Section 3.
  • Retention. The on-device landmark data exists only in memory during the animation and is discarded immediately. We do not retain your photo on our servers after the search (we have no servers that store it — see Section 2).
  • Sharing. Face landmark data is never shared with anyone. The photo is shared only with Google Cloud Vision, only to perform the search you requested, only after you consent.

2. No account required

FaceSeek does not have user accounts. There is no registration, sign-in, password, or profile. We do not ask for your name, email address, or phone number, and we do not create a personal account for you. Because we do not run our own server that stores your photos, we do not keep your submitted photos after a search completes.

3. Photos you submit and the third-party search service (consent)

When you choose a photo to run a search, that photo is sent directly from your device to Google Cloud Vision, a service operated by Google LLC, so its Web Detection feature can find public web pages where the same or a visually similar image appears. The first time you run a search, FaceSeek shows you an in-app consent screen that states exactly what is sent (only the chosen photo), who it is sent to (Google Cloud Vision / Google LLC), and what it is used for. No photo is sent until you agree.

We use the result only to show you the matching pages you requested. We do not operate our own server that stores your photos, and FaceSeek does not save your submitted photos after the search. Google processes the image as a data processor under Google Cloud's terms; see Google's Privacy Policy at https://policies.google.com/privacy and the Google Cloud Vision documentation for how Google handles request data. Your photo is used solely to perform the search you initiate; it is not used by FaceSeek to build any face database, identify you, or profile any individual.

4. On-device processing

As described in Section 1, any face-landmark detection happens entirely on your device using Apple's Vision framework and is never transmitted off the device.

5. Device permissions

  • Camera — used only if you choose to capture a new photo for a search.
  • Photo Library — used only to let you select an existing photo for a search.
  • Notifications (optional) — if you allow them, used to alert you about your searches; you can decline or disable them at any time in iOS Settings.

We access the camera and photo library only for the photo you actively choose to search. We do not scan or upload your photo library in the background.

6. Subscriptions and purchases

Subscriptions are processed by Apple through the App Store. We use RevenueCat to manage subscription status and entitlements. RevenueCat identifies your subscription using an anonymous, randomly generated app-user identifier; it is not linked to your real-world identity. We and RevenueCat receive purchase and subscription status from Apple (for example, whether your subscription is active) but not your Apple ID password or full payment card details. See RevenueCat's privacy policy at https://www.revenuecat.com/privacy.

7. Information we do NOT collect

FaceSeek does not collect your location, contacts, health data, or browsing history, and does not use third-party advertising trackers or App Tracking Transparency tracking. We do not sell your personal data.

8. Third-party services

  • Google Cloud Vision (Google LLC) — reverse-image / web detection on the photo you submit, performed only after your consent.
  • RevenueCat, Inc. — subscription management (anonymous identifier).
  • Apple — App Store in-app purchases.

We confirm that the third party we share the photo with (Google Cloud Vision) is contractually bound to protect it as a data processor and provides protections equivalent to those described in this policy.

9. No profiling of individuals

FaceSeek is a reverse-image search. It returns links to public web pages where an image appears; it does not aggregate information from those pages, does not assemble a profile of any individual, does not name or identify people, and does not access government, criminal, background-check, dating, or other restricted databases. Results are probabilistic links to public web pages.

10. Acceptable use

FaceSeek searches publicly available content and is intended for lawful uses such as checking where your own photos appear online or reverse-searching an image you have the right to search. FaceSeek is not an identity-verification, background-check, people-search, or surveillance product and must not be used to identify, harass, stalk, track, or harm anyone. Verify before acting on any result.

11. Children

FaceSeek is not directed to children. It is intended for users aged 17 and older, and we do not knowingly collect data from children.

12. Your choices and rights

Because we do not maintain accounts or store your photos on our servers, there is no account data for us to export or delete. You can stop using the app at any time, manage or cancel your subscription in your Apple ID settings, and revoke camera, photo, or notification permissions in iOS Settings. To ask a question about your data, contact us below.

13. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date above.
